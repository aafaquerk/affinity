Hi. This is Aafaque R. Khan.

I am a Doctoral Candiate and NASA Future Investigator on FINESST Grant at Steward Observatory. I build space telescopes and instruments to study and explore the universe.

Current Projects

Aspera
NASA Pioneers SmallSat

Aspera SmallSat will study the diffuse O VI emission at 103.2 nm from the warm-hot gas in the circumgalactic medium (CGM) of nearby galaxies.

FIREBall-2
Statospheric Balloon Mission

FIREBall-2 is a 1-m NUV telescope with a multi-object spectrograph designed to study redshifted emission from gas in CGM of nearby galaxies.

Dark Current in Sillicon Detectors

Test setup to charachterize the Noise charachteristics of Sillicon based arrays using single photon detection capabilities of EMCCD detectors

Electron Multiplying Charged Couple Detectors (EMCCDs)

Exploring applications of single photon counting capablities in UV Astronomy. Developing EMCCD detectors for future space missions.

Patterned Atomic Layer Deposition (ALD) coatings

Developing delta-doped EMCCDS with high UV Quantum Efficiency (QE) with direct deposited anti-reflection (AR) coatings using ALD techniques.

Skipper CCDs for Ultravoilet Astronomy

Developing and Charachterisation of UV enhanced delta-doped Skipper CCDs detectors for applications in space-based UV astronomy.

Vacuum UV Quantum Efficiency Measurement Setup

Developing automated detector charachterisation test setup to measure noise and throughput performance of experimental UV detectors.

Detecting UV Transients
with NUTRANS

Upgrating detector systems for SuperLOTIS, a 60 cm robotic telescope on Kitt Peak, to observe transients in the Near Ultravoilet.

Optical Alignment
and Metrology

Application of Interferometery, Computer Generataed Holograms and 3D blue light photogrammetery for optical alimgent and metrology.

ISRO's Aditya L1 Mission

Aditya-L1 is a solar coronagraph mission to study the solar corona. The was launched on 2nd September 2023 on a PSVL into a halo orbit around the L1, which is 1.5 million km from the Earth. The mission will carry seven payloads to study the solar corona and the chromosphere.
